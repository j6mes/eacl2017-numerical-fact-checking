What time does Northern Ireland v Germany kick off? What TV channel is it on? What are the odds?

Michael O'Neill's men are in with a shout of a stunning progression to the last 16

  •  Share
  •  Comments
ByPhil Smith
  • 06:00, 21 JUN 2016
  • Updated18:53, 20 JUN 2016
Sport
  •  Share
  •  Comments

Northern Ireland try to seal an unlikely spot in the last 16 of Euro 2016 as they take on Germany.

The World Champions have been well short of their best so far but are sitting comfortably on four points.

Here's everything you need to know...

What time is kick-off?

It's a 5pm start.

The game takes place in the Parc des Princes, Paris, which has a capacity of 48,712.

Where can I watch it?

The match is live on BBC 1.

Coverage starts at 4.30 pm.

Gary Lineker is presenting with Alan Shearer, Neil Lennon and Jens Lehmann in the studio.

Commentary is from Jonathan Pearce and Gerry Armstrong.

Likely line-ups?

Northern Ireland (4-5-1): Michael McGovern (Hamilton), Aaron Hughes (Unattached), Craig Cathcart (Watford), Gareth McAuley (West Brom), Jonny Evans (West Brom), Jamie Ward (Nottingham Forest), Corry Evans (Blackburn Rovers), Steven Davis (Southampton), Oliver Norwood (Reading), Stuart Dallas (Leeds United), Conor Washington (QPR)

Germany (4-2-3-1): Manuel Neuer (Bayern), Benedikt Howedes (Schalke 04), Jerome Boateng (Bayern), Mats Hummels (Bayern), Jonas Hector (FC Koln), Sami Khedira (Juventus), Toni Kroos (Real Madrid), Thomas Muller (Bayern), Mesut Ozil (Arsenal), Julian Draxler (Wolfsburg), Mario Gotze (Bayern)

Who's the ref?

In charge is the Frenchman Clement Turpin.

What are the bookies saying?

Unsurprisingly, Germany are overwhelming favourites at 3/10. The draw is at 5/1 while a Northern Ireland win is all the way out at 14/1.
