Home Office 'failure' over foreign offenders 'underming confidence in immigration system'
June 3, 2016

A Home Affairs Committee report says the continued failure by the Home Office to remove the equivalent of a "small town" of Foreign National Offenders (FNOs) remaining in the UK's prisons and communities is undermining confidence in the immigration system, and in the UK's EU membership.

Progress is too slow and the number of FNOs still in the UK is, at over 13,000, "the size of a small town", with many being housed in prisons at great expense. The Home Office must immediately set out the practical steps which it will take to "significantly" reduce this figure or see the Committee set its own targets to be monitored in future quarterly reports.

Community Channel in £300,000 community share issue bid
June 2, 2016

The Community Channel plans to become the UK’s first community-owned TV channel after offering charities and members of the public the chance to own a stake. The Community Channel is owned by the Media Trust and focuses the third sector and charities. It is offering people the opportunity to buy community shares issued via the crowdfunding platform Crowdfunder.

 

 

"We want to be a channel of the community, by the community and for the community – independent, creatively renewed, and committed to being not-for-profit," says the Media Trust.

Refugee Action in search for chair
June 1, 2016

 

 

 

 

 

 

Refugee Action – the  national that charity plays a critical role in tackling the issues faced by refugees and asylum seekers across the UK - is on the look-out for a new chair. The charity has adopted an ambitious new strategy to try to make an impact over the next few years, alongside a new approach to achieving its goals.

 

RA is looking for a chair with

  • a deep commitment to the rights of asylum seekers and refugees,
  • considerable governance experience,
  • substantial leadership experience
  • a track record of positive impact in the voluntary sector or public life.
