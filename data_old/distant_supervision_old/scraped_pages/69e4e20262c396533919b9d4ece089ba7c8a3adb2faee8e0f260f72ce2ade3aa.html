About the Author

I am a writer, wife and mother. I live in SW lower Michigan near the banks of Brandywine Creek. I adore writing historical and contemporary stories, facing the challenges that life throws at you with characters that are relate-able. I love finding humor in the ridiculous things that are in the everyday comings and goings of life. For me a good book is when you get to step into the character's shoes and join them on their journey. So climb aboard, lets share the adventure!

3.0 out of 5 starsSecond half much better than the first, so stick with it
By Nastassja on November 25, 2014
Format: Kindle Edition Verified Purchase
The first half of the story read more like the heroine's diary during her time on "The Bachelor", the story and momentum is very choppy at first. The first half is also a prime example of the author telling, not showing. I got quite frustrated and ended up skipping some chunks because I found it really boring to read about how the h sat around reading while the other girls went out on dates. Her dates with the H were also not nearly detailed enough to get me to connect with said bachelor, though the reason why became obvious later on. Still, more than one paragraph telling me they talked while taking a moonlit stroll would have been nice. Don't tell my they talked, let me be a part of their conversation! Having said that, the second half of the book is MUCH better than the first. I enjoyed the characters and the character development, and it made up for the disappointing first half. The writing is so very different, it's almost as if a different author wrote the second half. I do appreciated that the author delivered a story that was quite unpredictable at times. I love the choices the heroine made in the second half of the book - it showed an understanding and compassionate yet strong and wise woman. She came a long way

3.0 out of 5 starsGreat story outweighed the horrible grammar
By Karis Brody on April 25, 2013
Format: Kindle Edition Verified Purchase
Almost every sentence has some sort of typo, grammatical error, or writer's "no-no." Several times, I had to reread a sentence just to understand what she was trying to say. Misplaced commas and the incorrect use of quotation marks made reading choppy and laborious. She flips from present tense to past tense to future tense, all in one paragraph. Pick ONE and stick to it! Also, there is a spot mid-book where she briefly switches from first person point of view to third person. Overall, this book desperately begs for an editor!

I usually don't take the time to complete books so childishly written. But, this book redeems itself with a stellar plot line and lovable characters!!! I quickly fell in love with the characters and believable situations. I was willing to tough through the grammatical errors, simply to find out what happened with Hannah and Jackson!

Leah has great ideas and seems to be a true novelist at heart. She just needs an editor to polish up her very creative stories.
