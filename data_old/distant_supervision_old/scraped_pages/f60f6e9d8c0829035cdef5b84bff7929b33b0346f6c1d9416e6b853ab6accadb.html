Liverpool local elections 2016 Thursday recap: Mayor Joe Anderson re-elected plus results from councils across Merseyside

Council seats up for grabs in your area, along with Liverpool Mayoral election and Police and Crime Commissioner poll

  •  Share
ByLiam MurphyLorna HughesSteve GravesTom BelgerJade Pike
  • Updated06:05, 6 MAY 2016
News
  •  Share

Counts are taking place in Sefton, Liverpool, Wirral, St Helens and Halton overnight.

Others are happening in the morning, with a third of all seats in most metropolitan boroughs including Liverpool, Sefton, St Helens and Wirral up for grabs, while other elections including the London Mayoral contest, Welsh Assembly and Scottish Parliament polls will also take place on the same day.

In Merseyside there are contests in all council areas, as well as the Liverpool Mayoral race and the election for the Police and Crime Commissioner role.

All seats are up for election in Wirral, Knowsley, Warrington and West Lancashire.

Comparisons with 2012

So how does Mayor Anderson’s victory this time compare to the picture four years ago? Well his overall share of the vote has gone down, but it’s still a resounding first-round victory. Part of the drop can be explained by an improved performance for Lib Dem leader Richard Kemp, who got 14,000 more votes this time than last.

Of course back in 2012 the Liberal Democrats were in coalition with the Conservatives nationally and many local politicians bore the brunt of public anger at the national picture.

Results from around the UK

Early results from the “Super Thursday” elections suggested Labour was facing a tough night, with the party slipping back in English councils and facing the prospect of being forced into third place in Scotland.

With 45 million people eligible to vote, the elections were being seen as the first nationwide verdict on Jeremy Corbyn’s leadership of the party since he stormed to victory with massive grassroots support last September.

Scottish Conservative leader Ruth Davidson said her party was “confident” of securing second place in elections to the Scottish Parliament, which Mr Corbyn identified as a top priority shortly after his election.

On the eve of polling, Labour retreated from comments its leader made in the final days of the campaign that the party was on course for gains in the local authority elections.

And though it held on to council strongholds like Sunderland and Newcastle-upon-Tyne and the bellwether authority Nuneaton, and improved its vote share to hold Sheffield Brightside in a Westminster by-election, there were early signs that Labour was heading for a reduced tally of councillors across England.

Sefton - Labour loses three seats to community activists

Labour lost three seats to community activists in Sefton - after a count which saw their total vote plunge 44% in the borough.

The party, which retains control of the local authority despite a poor night, lost in Formby’s Ravenmeols, Park and Sudell wards to independent candidates and a community action party.

St Helens council election results: few surprises as Labour hold convincing majority

Labour held on comfortably to their majority on St Helens council in last night’s local elections .

There were few surprises on the night as Labour won the 15 seats they currently hold, and the Conservatives and Lib Dems held their one seat each.

UKIP finished second in nine wards, but failed to translate their popularity into any actual seats.

The council’s Labour leader claimed the party had taken some votes that previously went to the British National Party (BNP) in the area.

02:45KEY EVENT

Wirral stays Labour

Wirral Council has been under the firm control of Labour since the local elections in 2012 – and it remains the case with no seats changing hands in the borough.
Labour continues to hold 39 of the council’s 66 seats while the Conservatives have 21, the Liberal Democrats have five and the Green Party have one seat, in Birkenhead & Tranmere.
Labour defended 14 seats tonight, the Tories 7 and the Lib Dems 2.

Sefton surprises

A night of surprises in Sefton! First, Labour councillor Nina Killen loses her Sudell seat to independent Matthew Gannon, before ‘Pat from Lydiate’ - aka Pat O’Hanlon, a residents’ group campaigner who has led on green belt land - took Park from Labour.

And in Southport, the Lib Dems took all seven seats, clawing back Dukes ward from the Tories.
Overall tonight, in the 23 contested seats, Labour took 12, the Lib Dems 7, the Tories 1 and others 3.
