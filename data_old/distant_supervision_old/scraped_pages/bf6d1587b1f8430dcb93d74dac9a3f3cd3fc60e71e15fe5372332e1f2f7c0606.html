Why go to Florida?

With powdery sands, coconut palms and magical family attractions, Florida deserves its dreamy reputation. Summer here seems to last forever, perhaps because local life moves at the pace of a lazy Southern drawl.

Fun is serious business around these parts, though, with enough theme parks to keep the kids wired for weeks, as well as 2,170 kilometres (1,350 miles) of glorious coastline to explore. Throw in alligator farms, tropical islands and space centres, and youve got a humdinger of a holiday on your hands.

Abandon your adulthood


Holidays to Florida scream Walt Disney World theres no dodging that fact. Even the staunchest of skeptics should fall for the larger-than-life charms of the Magic Kingdom, complete with stomach-flipping rides and rainbow fireworks fizzing over Cinderellas castle.

Searing heat and snaking queues dont even begin to dent the wide-eyed excitement of Disney World, so prepare to be spellbound. Nearby, youll also find SeaWorld, Universal Studios, Epcot, Gatorland, the Kennedy Space Center and LEGOLAND Florida Resort.

Explore the secret Sunshine State


Florida holidays are definitely not just about theme parks, however. Beyond the movie sets and water slides of Orlando, this sultry southern state is a natural playground criss-crossed by huge swathes of untamed wilderness.

The steamy swamps and tangled mangroves of the Everglades National Park cover a whopping 1.5 million acres of Floridas mainland, while the Keys boast the only living coral barrier reef in the United States and the coastline is peppered with white-sand beaches. 

Discover a cultural cocktail


Florida may be famous for its novelty attractions, but its local culture is as fascinating as its landscape. From the fiery Latino vibes of Miami to the swanky sands of South Beach and the arty corners of Key West, a holiday to Florida is full of surprises.

Its Latin America and Deep South influences mean you will find a cornucopia of music, literature and mouth-watering cuisine. 


When to go


If youre heading to Florida on holiday in 2017, you are pretty much guaranteed gorgeous weather its not called the Sunshine State for nothing. Peak season in South Florida stretches from December to April, when the so-called snowbirds migrate from the chilly northern states.

Disney World and the other theme parks become a merry, sweaty squish as soon as the schools are out for summer, drawing crowds that are likely to madden even the most Zen parents. 

If youre on the hunt for a cheap holiday deal to Florida, try travelling between September and November, when there are refreshingly few tourists and the heat has mellowed out after the summer. Hotel prices tumble during these months, giving you the chance to snap up a cheap Florida package deal at a fraction of the usual price.

If youve got designs on Disney World, you can stumble upon some great 2017 Florida holidays between January and the Easter break, when the weather is mild and most kids are at school. 

Events for your diary


Carnaval Miami: Join the worlds longest congo line in Miamis carnival, a two-week fiesta that celebrates everything Latino and stretches across 23 blocks of Little Havana in mid-March.

Stone Crab Festival: Find yourself elbow deep in crab claws during Floridas Stone Crab Festival in Naples, held every October. Its a seafood feast and local knees-up all rolled into one.
