Why did no one clap for "bag lady" Jenny Beavan when she collected her Oscar? Viewers criticise stars

  • 23:06, 29 Feb 2016
  • Updated 08:26, 1 Mar 2016
  • By Zoe Shenton

Video shows a number of audience members failing to applaud the dressed-down costume designer as she went to accept her Oscar

    A who's who of Hollywood were celebrated at Sunday night's 88th Academy Awards.

    But for one star - British costume designer Jenny Beavan - the celebration appeared to be muted.

    Jenny, who was branded a "bag lady" by Stephen Fry just last week, picked up the award for Best Costume Design for her work on Mad Max: Fury Road and as she walked to the stage, those around her - including Revenant director Alejandro G Inarritu - failed to clap.

    Spotlight director Tom McCarthy had his hand over his mouth as she made her way to the stage while others just looked blankly on or whispered to those sat next to them.

    The designer, dressed down for the occasion in a leather Marks and Spencer jacket, loose-fitting black trousers and a red scarf, appeared to be unfazed by the lack of applause and graciously accepted her award.

    Viewers at home soon took to Twitter to blast those caught on camera, with one sharing: "Really though - what was up with EVERY DUDE that Jenny Beavan walked passed no clapping for her?"

    And: "Oscars low point: the salty patriarchy not even clapping for Jenny Beaven."

    Another wrote: "Seriously though, what's up with folks not applauding for Jenny Beaven?"

    However, others were quick to point out that a lot of those people were clapping by the time she reached the stage.

    Meanwhile, last week BAFTA host Stephen Fry came under fire for calling Jenny a "bag lady".

    The 58-year-old actor-and-writer made the gag in reference to what Jenny wore to the ceremony after she collected yet another award for Costume Design for her work on Mad Max: Fury Road .

    As Beavan left the stage, Fry quipped: "Only one of the great cinematic costume designers would come to the awards ceremony dressed like a bag lady."

    His comment drew an avalanche of criticism from Twitter users who were quick to condemn the comic star for what he said.
