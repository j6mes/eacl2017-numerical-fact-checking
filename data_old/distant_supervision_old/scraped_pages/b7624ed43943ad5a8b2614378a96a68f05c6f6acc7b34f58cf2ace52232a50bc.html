SHS girls bowl to runner-up trophy

Lady Pioneers show well in D3
BY DALE BALL
Sports Editor

The Sandusky girls bowling team brought home the Division 4 Team runner-up trophy. Photo by Kevin Gregory It was a very good day on the lanes for two Sanilac County girls teams at the MHSAA Team State Bowling Finals on Friday.

Sandusky, a traditional state-power, finished as runner-up at Division 4 tournament at Sunnybrook Lanes in Sterling Heights, losing to No. 1 ranked Vandercook Lake in the championship match.

Meanwhile, Cros-Lex emerged as the No. 1 qualifier at the Division 3 finals at Airport Lanes in Jackson before falling in the first round of the playoffs.

The Lady Redskins’ bowling program has won two State Team titles, including last year, and now has three runner up trophies in the 10- year history of the MHSAA sponsored event.

It took another powerhouse to stop Sandusky this time. Vandercook Lake, which defeated the Lady ‘Skins, 1322-1166 in the championship match on Friday, now has four State crowns and three second place finishes

The Jayhawks, who have four girls averaging near 200 this season, built a 382-354, lead after the two Baker games and used a strong, late finish for 940 team game to pull away to the title.

The final score, though, was deceiving. Coach Gordy Williams of the Lady Redskins explained. “We were in good shape until the last three or four frames. We were only down about six pins. Then, we all started to open and they all started to strike.”

Williams added that his team doesn’t need to hang their heads.

“We made it to the finals, and that’s where we needed to be,” he said. “(Vandercook Lake) is a good team. We expect to run into them, at one point, every year.”

Mallory Ambs, who went on to win the State Singles title on Saturday, led Vandercook Lake with a 225 game. The Jayhawks had three other bowlers that notched 182 or better. Sophomore Keri Molloy led the Lady ‘Skins in the team game with a 187. Sisters Stephanie and Melissa Sleda added games of 171 and 170. Freshman Caroline Ross shot a 156.

“I’m really happy with how the team did,” Williams added. “We made it a long ways. Runner-up is not bad. And, all our girls bowled well.”

In the qualifying block earlier in the day, Vandercook Lake and Sandusky finished onetwo and were locked in opposite brackets for the playoffs.

The Lady ‘Skins opened with a win over Bad Axe, 1132-1027, in the quarterfinals, and followed with a, 1225-1108, victory over Ravenna in the semis. Vandercook Lake, with a total pin count of 3456 in the qualifying, easily defeated Grass Lake and Portland St. Patrick in its first two playoff matches.

At Jackson, the Lady Pioneers surprised the field when they posted a 3040 score in the qualifying round to lock down the No. 1 seed. A 1040 score in the final team game vaulted Cros-Lex past several other teams.

Cros-Lex then saw its day end in the quarterfinals with a narrow seven pin, 1121-1114, loss to Ishpeming. Battle Creek Pennfield, the No. 6 seed after qualifying, went on to win the D3 State title.
